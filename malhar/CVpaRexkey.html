यूनिवर्सिटीज़ ऑफ़ विर्जिनिया ऐंड मिशिगन

Key to exercise on compound verbs in  पड़-

     1a.  चेचक फूट पड़ी।       'Smallpox broke out.'   (see Bahri's  श्ािक्षाथीर् हिन्दी- अँग्रेज़ी श्ाब्दकोश्ा.)
     1b.  बोतल फूट गई।       'The bottle broke.'
     Explanation: In (1b) the event of bursting leads to the destruction of the bottle. It "goes away".  In (1a) the breaking out of the disease leads to its presence, not its absence.  Compare remarks about  लौट पड़  versus  लौट जा  in notes.
     2.   किताब मिल गई !       'Found the book!'
     Explanation (speculative):  The event of getting or finding denoted by मिल- can only lead to the presence of the book, not to its absence. Vector  जा's residual sense of going away is canceled out and with it the need to express a contrast by using vector पड़.
     3a.  मैं   ...   जान गया कि  ...       'I learnt from his sister that he was sick.'
     3b.  मुझे   ...    जान पड़ा कि ...       'I learnt from his sister that he was sick.'   (see Bahri's  श्ाब्दकोश्ा.)
     3c.  मैं ने   ...   जान लिया कि  ...       'I learnt from his sister that he was sick.'
     Explanation: In this set of three the distribution of vectors is determined by grammar.  In (3b) the use of vector  पड़  is the remnant of an old passive.  The phrase  जान पड़  is now a fixed expression which means 'seem' or (in 3b) 'learn' or 'find out' and which requires  को  of experiencer (= the person who found out).  The construction in (3a) is straightforward.  Use of vector  जा  implies the learning or finding out did not involve effort.  Contrast (3a) with the more deliberate  जान लिया  of (3c).
     4a.  दादा की गाड़ी ... बोल जाएगी।
            'Grampa's car (was so bad it) looked as if at any moment it would give up the ghost.'
     4b.  दादा की तसवीर ... बोल पड़ेगी।
            'Grampa's portrait (was so good it) looked as if at any moment it would speak.'
     Explanation (speculative):  Does the existence of a special idiom  बोल जा  'break down entirely; stop working' favor the use of vector  पड़  when commoner meanings of  बोल  are intended?
     5a.  राय साहब का मुँह गिर गया।       (from  गोदान )
            'Ray Sahib's face fell.'
     5b.  बच्चा मुँह के बल गिर पड़ा  /  गया।      (adapted from  गोदान )
            'The boy fell on his face.'
     Explanation:  If  गिर पड़ा  were used in (5a) the sense would be something like 'Ray Sahib's face fell down', like a mango from a tree!
     6a.  सेना श्ाहर पर टूट पड़ी ...       'Like a tiger the army fell on the city.'
     6b.  खोपड़ी पत्थर पर टूट गई ...       'Like a teacup the coconut broke on the rock.'
     Explanation:  The role of subject in the idiomatic expression  टूट पड़- 'attack' is an unlikely one for a coconut.
     7a. ... अाँखों से ... अाँसू निकल पड़े।            (from  गोदान )
           '...tears of joy flowed from Govindi's eyes.'
     7b. ... रुपए हाथ से निकल गए ...             (from  गोदान )
            '... if I lose the money, I'll be ruined.'
     Explanation:  It is possible to use  गए  in (7a) as well as the  पड़े  that  प्रेमचन्द  chose.  However, in (7b) using  पड़े  instead of  गए  would work against the sense of loss.  Compare a similar explanation for the  गई  in (1b).
     8.  वह पुलिस के सामने फूट पड़ा।      'He revealed everything to the police.'     (see Bahri's  श्ाब्दकोश्ा.)
     Explanation:  The use of vector  जा  instead of  पड़  would denote an actual physical explosion.
     9a. गाड़ी निकल गई । एक–एक करके कुली स्टेशन के बाहर चले गए । प्लेटफार्म पर मौन छा गया ।   (from भीष्म साहनी's story "माता–विमाता")
           'The train left. One by one the porters went out of the station. Silence spread over the platform.'
     9b. फटाफट पानी के दो बाॅटल और एक काॅफी लेकर वापस आ ही रहे थे कि ट्रेन निकल पड़ी ।
            हमें जल्दी से दूसरी बोगी में चढ़ना पड़ा।                         (from yayawari-sanjiv.blogspot.com/2015/06 )
            'We were hurrying back with two bottles of water and a coffee when the train started.
             We had to quickly get into a different bogey.'
     Explanation: In (9a) the train leaves the scene but we as readers are still at the station. In (9b) we are back [just barely]
                           on board the train. Compare a similar explanation for निकल पड़ी in (10) in the notes.
