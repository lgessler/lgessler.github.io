Passive to indicate action by unspecified subjects

      In Hindi-Urdu the inability or unwillingness to specify the subject of an action may lead speakers to use the passive, even if the verb is intransitive:

  1.  पड़ोस के घर से सुई-तागा लेने दौड़ा जा रहा है।
     'People are running to get a needle and thread from the neighbors.'

     (from § 1 of  प्रेमचन्द's  ईदगाह. See context.)

Because the passive of an intransitive verb has nothing to agree with it always assumes the default masculine third person singular form in -अा:

  2.  तेली के घर भागा जाता है।
     'People are running to the oil-man's house.'

     (from § 1 of  प्रेमचन्द's  ईदगाह. See context.)

      This use of the passive of intransitives is not common.  For other, more common uses, see notes on  रहा नहीं जाता and the passive of incapacity.
